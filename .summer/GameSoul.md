## Vision
This is a tower defense game built around stillness and sharp thinking. Clean geometry, quiet focus, and the satisfying feeling of cracking a puzzle wide open. It is the kind of game that rewards patience and careful placement over reflexes and chaos.

## Genre and Inspirations
You are drawn to tower defense as a genre, and you are coming to it fresh without strong reference points pulling you in any one direction. That is actually a gift. It means your game will not be a remix of something familiar. You are free to find your own version of what tower defense can feel like, starting from first principles rather than imitation.

## Look and Feel
The visual world is **pure geometry**: grids, clean lines, shapes that mean exactly what they are. No decorative clutter, no story told through environmental art. The interface and the game are almost the same thing. Colors would likely be restrained, maybe a cool neutral palette with a single accent color that signals danger or opportunity. The sound design matches: soft ambient tones, satisfying placement clicks, music that fades into the background and helps you think rather than trying to excite you.

## Core Mechanics
Every level presents a puzzle with an optimal solution waiting to be discovered. Players place towers on an abstract grid and must figure out not just *where* to put them, but in what order and combination. As the game progresses, towers evolve into meaningfully different versions of themselves through upgrades, opening up new strategies and sometimes invalidating old ones.

The player experience moment-to-moment looks like this:
- Survey the grid and incoming wave information
- Place towers deliberately, thinking several moves ahead
- Watch the solution either hold or reveal a flaw
- Upgrade key towers at the right moment to shift the outcome
- Refine, restart, or discover that a surprising arrangement actually works better

## What Makes It Special
Most tower defense games create tension through speed and spectacle. Yours creates tension through **clarity and constraint**. The abstract grid setting removes all narrative noise and puts the puzzle itself front and center. Combined with deep upgrade paths, this means players are not just defending against enemies, they are uncovering the hidden logic of each level. There is a quiet elegance to that, closer to a logic puzzle or a game of chess than to most action-adjacent strategy games. That is a genuinely distinct corner of the genre worth exploring.
